About this Project

So this started when I decided I wanted to code. I have some experience of working in a couple of different frameworks: Magento, Shopify and most extensively Wordpress. I have become accustomed to adding custom CSS or modifying pre-existing template files to get the results I wanted, but have never had any formal training in the basics. I felt I needed to understand WHY the things that I knew worked, worked. I also wanted to get some understanding of the syntax of the languages I knew a bit about (html and CSS mainly) and to learn more languages - javascript has always been a bit of a mystery to me.

One of the first problems I ran up against in my attempts to make my practice websites, was the lack of decent image resources. Yes I could hotlink a lot of stuff from Google, but I didn't like the implications of using images without the prior consent of the creators, and didn't have time to source a million different images that still had a stylistic theme to them. So I asked for help.

One of my most recent hobbies has been nail polish /nail art. There's something quite addictive for any collector about the huge range of colours and finishes out there, all in a handy small package - plus... pretty nails. Nail polish/art photos tend to be stylisticly similar - there's only so many ways you can take photos of the tips of your fingers. This combined with the visually pleasing range of colours seemed like a great subject for a practice website. I asked the members of a Facebook Group called "Polish-aholics Anonymous" if they could help me with my project and was amazed at how kind and helpful the responses have been. I have credited the contributor's names every time I have used one of their images, but I wanted to take a moment and thank these generous and talented ladies
